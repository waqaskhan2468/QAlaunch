High Accessibility · WCAG 2.1 AA
Affecting all CTA buttons
Every primary call-to-action button fails contrast standards

All primary CTAs on the page (white text on teal #14b88f background) measure a 2.53:1 contrast ratio — far below the 4.5:1 required for WCAG AA compliance. This impacts 'Book 1:1 Session', 'Hire Me', 'Get Full Assessment', 'View All Articles', and the YouTube CTA. These are your primary conversion drivers.

⚡ Business Impact: Users with low vision or color deficiency cannot reliably read your CTA buttons. This is also a documented accessibility lawsuit risk under ADA Title III.
High Accessibility · Navigation
Affecting active page indicator
Active navigation link has unreadable contrast (2.49:1)

The active 'Home' nav link uses brand teal on near-white background — measured at 2.49:1, less than half the required 4.5:1. This is the first focusable element keyboard users hit and is the active-page indicator. Users relying on color to identify which page they're on cannot tell.

⚡ Business Impact: Keyboard and low-vision visitors lose orientation immediately upon arriving. They don't know which page they're on. Confusion increases bounce rate.
High Accessibility · Link Purpose
Affecting social media
YouTube footer link has no accessible name (invisible to screen readers)

The YouTube link in your footer contains only an icon — no visible text, no aria-label, no aria-labelledby, no title attribute. Screen readers announce this link as 'unlabeled' or 'empty', giving users no idea what they're clicking. Violates WCAG 2.1 SC 2.4.4 (Link Purpose) and SC 4.1.2 (Name, Role, Value).

⚡ Business Impact: Screen reader users cannot discover your YouTube channel through your website. Your social media is effectively invisible to a meaningful percentage of visitors.
